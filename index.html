Milestones of Hong Kong's Constitutional Development - Timeline

Timeline of Hong Kong


  • Pre-1842

    Small Fishing Village

    Hong Kong, a fishing community, is under China's rule. Nevertheless, at the time, Hong Kong was lightly populated and was largely ignored by Chinese officials.

  • August 29, 1842

    Treaty of Nanking

    On this date, the Treaty of Nanking was signed, marking the start of British Rule.

  • 1979

    MacLehose meets Deng

    At this time, China's Premier Deng Xiaoping invited Hong Kong's governor Murray MacLehose to Beijing. Deng told MacLehose that China might take over Hong Kong by 1997 but it would respect Hong Kong's special status.
    This meeting marked first ocassion high level talks on Hong Kong Sovereignty between Britain and Hong Kong.

  • June, 1982

    Thatcher meets Deng

    Chinese Premier Deng Xiaoing meets Britain's prime minister Margaret Thatcher. Deng stood firm on his intention of taking back Hong Kong. Thatcher tried to hold her line by saying that China would violate the international treaty if China tried to take the Hong Kong.

  • July 1, 1979

    Hong Kong Handover

    Hong Kong is officially handed over to the People's Republic of China. China pledges to take care of Hong Kong under their "one country, two systems" mantra.

The Hong Kong Post Office (HKPO) was established under British Control in 1841 solely for the use of the military.

In 1860, the jurisdiction over the Hong Kong Post Office was transferred from the British Post Office to the Hong Kong colonial government.

The first set of postage stamps, a set of Queen Victoria stamps, was issued on December 8, 1862. This marked

From 1880 - 1895, the HKPO released the Queen Victoria Issues Postal Cards, the first Hong Kong postal cards, with 1¢, 3¢, and 4¢ denominations. From 1841 - 1949, the HKPO published mainly British Royalties stamps.

Botanical Gardens
Opened in 1871 as Botanic Garden and renamed in 1975 as Botanical and Zoological Gardens to reflect the increased commitments to zoological exhibits.

Opened in 1977 at a cost of $150 million HK dollars. It was funded by the HK Jockey Club. Land was provided for free by the Hong Kong Government

Map of Country Parks

Country Parks
The Country Parks were established in the 1970s as a way of protecting and preserving water ways.
In 1976 the Country Parks Ordnance was written in to Hong Kong law, which specified that country parks in the region were designated for the purposes of :
1. encouraging their development and use for recreation and tourism (and providing facilities for this);
2. protecting vegetation and wildlife within the parks;
3. to preserve sites of historic or cultural significance
Sources: Country Parks History,  Country Parks List

Ports of Hong Kong Past and Present FDC (January 12th, 1982)

Ports of Hong Kong Past and Present FDC BACK

On January 6, 1982, Beijing’s Prime Minister, Zhao Ziyang, met with Britain’s Junior Minister Humphrey Atkins at Beijing. Atkins was responsible for Hong Kong at the Foreign Offices. In the meeting, Ziyang told Atkin that the PRC was making plans to maintain Hong Kong’s prosperity, stability, free ports, and business center.


Atkins expressed Britain’s willingness to open talks about Hong Kong’s sovereignty when he told Ziyang that confidence in Hong Kong would be endangered if the issue of Hong Kong sovereignty was not resolved.

World Bank Group/International Monetary Fund Annual Meeting (September 21, 1997)

This set of stamps commemorates the 1997 Annual Meetings of the Boards of Governors of the World Bank Group and the International Monetary Fund held in Hong Kong during the period of 23 to 25 September 1997. The stamps depict me of the services which help Hong Kong maintain its role as an international financial centre and service hub in Asia. They are: $1.30 stamp (finance and banking); $2.50 stamp (investment and stock exchange); $3.10 stamp (trade and telecommunications); $5.00 stamp (infrastructure and transport).


The World Bank Group consists of the International Bank of Reconstruction and Development, the International Development Association, the International Financial Corporation, the Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency, and the International Centre for the Settlement of Investment Disputes. The World Bank Group is a partner in strengthening economies and expanding markets to improve the quality of life for people everywhere, especially the poorest.


The main objectives of the International Monetary Fund are to promote international monetary cooperation and to facilitate the expansion of international trade and, through this, to contribute to the maintenance of high levels of employment and real income. To this end, the International Monetary Fund promotes exchange stability among currencies and a multilateral payment system, and provides temporary balance for payment financing to its member countries.


There are now 180 member countries of the World Bank Group and 181 of the International Monetary Fund. The World Bank Group and the International Monetary Fund hold joint meetings of their Bards of Governors annually. This is the 52nd Annual Meetings.

TOP ==> 10th Anniversary of the Establishment of the Special Administrative Region (July 1, 1997)


From June, 2007 HK Post website:


A set of commemorative stamps on "The 10th Anniversary of the Establishment of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region" will be issued on 1 July 2007 (Sunday). On the same day, a joint stamp issue with China Post on "The 10th Anniversary of the Reunification of Hong Kong with China" will also be released.


To celebrate this special occasion and to exemplify the successful implementation of "One Country, Two Systems", Hongkong Post will also produce a Limited Edition Prestige Stamp Album. The album will contain two stamp panes. One pane will include "A Symphony of Lights" hologram stamp sheetlet and a serviced First Day Cover affixed with a set of six stamps of "The 10th Anniversary of the Establishment of the HKSAR". The other will include a mini-pane and a serviced First Day Cover affixed with a HK$1.40 stamp and a RMB1.20 stamp of "The 10th Anniversary of the Reunification of Hong Kong with China". Information on how to order the Limited Edition Album will be announced in early July.


To make these special stamp issues even more meaningful, Hongkong Post will set aside a portion of the proceeds from the sale of the stamp products issued on 1 July 2007 as a charitable donation.
